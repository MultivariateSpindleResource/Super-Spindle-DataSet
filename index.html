A versatile multivariate image analysis pipeline reveals features of Xenopus extract spindles

Thank you for your interest in our work.

Heald Lab has been around for a while...

And as cell biologists do, we tend to generate a lot of imaging data.

As we were going through the many hard drives of our images, we wondered - "how could we make it open to the general research community?". This is how this project was born.

With this publication we would like to share the thousands of images we collected over the past ~5 years, as well as propose a solution, which allows to quantify multiple characteristics about the captured spindle objects. We also suggest some analysis approaches, to help in narrowing down potential metrics (features/variables) of interest. We are basically presenting here a biological big (or medium) data-driven hypothesis generator, which should help launching cell biology researchers onto new project tracks.

Feel free to download the complete content of this repository (image processing pipelines, quantification data, code, instructions, example images) by clicking one of the buttons above. The actual data will be hosted by our fiends at Xenbase.
